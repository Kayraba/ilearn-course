Use the keyboard
================

01. Lesson 4: Use the keyboard. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, the keyboard becomes your tool for writing. We will type slowly, use Space between words, Enter for a new line, and Backspace to fix a mistake. Good typing is careful, not fast.

03. The keyboard has lots of keys, but three do most of the work: Space, Enter, and Backspace. Let us find them together.

04. We click where the line is flashing, and then we type, slowly. Let us type the word cat, one letter at a time.

05. The Space bar makes a gap between words. And if we make a mistake, the Backspace key removes the last letter. Mistakes are completely fine.

06. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Staff will name letters from the your name or a familiar word. You point first, then presses. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Use simple words such as cat, bus and tea. After each word, press Space before the next word. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type the word: cat. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Practise holding Shift and pressing one letter. Keep this calm; it is a stretch task. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

07. Here is one useful thing to watch for. Holding a key down types the same letter over and over. A quick tap gives one letter. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

08. Great effort. You can type words, use Space and Enter, and fix mistakes with Backspace. Mistakes are always fine. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
