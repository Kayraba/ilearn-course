Open, minimise and close
========================

01. Lesson 5: Open, minimise and close. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we look at windows. A program opens inside a window, and the buttons at the top help us minimise, maximise, or close it. We will practise choosing the right button calmly.

03. Double-click the blue W to open Word. Watch how it opens up in its own window on the screen.

04. Here it is, open in its own window. At the top corner are three small buttons that control the window.

05. When we are finished, we find the X in the very top corner. The X means: I am done with this window.

06. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Point to minimise, maximise and close. Explain that X means finished with this window. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Open one app, wait, minimise it, bring it back, then close it. Repeat slowly. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type the word for the button that closes a window. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Staff gives the four actions in order. You attempts each before staff prompts. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

07. Here is one useful thing to watch for. The X closes the window. The minus only tucks it away. Look carefully before you click. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

08. Nicely done. You can open a program, tuck it away, bring it back, and close it with the X when you are finished. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
