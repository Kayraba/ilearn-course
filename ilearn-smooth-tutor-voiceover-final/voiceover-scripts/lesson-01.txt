Turn on and sign in
===================

01. Lesson 1: Turn on and sign in. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, I will guide you through the full start-up routine. We press the power button once, wait calmly, sign in carefully, and check that the desktop is ready. There is no rush — waiting is part of using a computer well.

03. Look at the front or side of the computer for a small round button with a line through the top. That is the power button.

04. We press the power button once. Just once — a single, gentle press. Then we take our hand away and wait.

05. Waiting is part of using a computer well. The screen may be dark for a moment. That is completely normal. We stay calm and let it wake up.

06. Now the computer asks who we are. We type our password carefully, letter by letter. The dots hide it so it stays private.

07. And there it is. This screen is called the desktop. It is our starting place, with our folders and programs ready to use.

08. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Check the device is charged, the keyboard and mouse are connected, and you can sit comfortably with the screen at a good distance. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Complete the full start-up routine twice. First time with staff prompts, second time with fewer prompts. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type a practice password to sign in. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Open the Start menu, find the power option, and identify Shut down. Staff can complete the final click if needed. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

09. Here is one useful thing to watch for. Pressing the power button many times does not make it faster. One press, then wait. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

10. Brilliant. You now know how to turn a computer on, wait calmly, and sign in safely — the very first step to being confident. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
