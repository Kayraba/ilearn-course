Practice: save and find
=======================

01. Lesson 8: Practice: save and find. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we put the routine together. You will open Word, type a note, save it, close it, and find it again. This is a real independence skill, and we will take it one step at a time.

03. Watch closely just once. In a moment you will try the whole thing yourself, so notice each step as I go.

04. First we type our note.

05. Then we save it, with a name, in Documents.

06. And finally we find it again in the Documents folder. This whole routine is where independence begins to show.

07. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Create, type, save, close, find. Staff will say it once, You repeats or points to prompt cards. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Open Word, type a note, save it in Documents, close Word, then reopen the file. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type a short note. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Use words, pointing or yes/no choices. Staff notes barriers and the next support step. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

08. Here is one useful thing to watch for. Try not to rush. Saying each step out loud first makes the whole routine easier. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

09. That was excellent. Create, save, close, and find again — you put the whole routine together. This is real independence. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
