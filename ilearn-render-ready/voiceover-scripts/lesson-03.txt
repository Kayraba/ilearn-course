Open with double-click
======================

01. Lesson 3: Open with double-click. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we learn double-clicking. Two quick clicks can open a program or file, but the mouse needs to stay still. If it does not open first time, that is fine — we pause, reset, and try again.

03. Point the arrow at the Documents folder. To open it, we will use two quick clicks, one after the other.

04. The secret is this: keep the mouse perfectly still, and click twice quickly. Click, click. The stillness is what matters most.

05. And it opens. Well done. That is a double-click. The Documents folder is now open in front of us.

06. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: You practise two quick taps on the mouse while keeping the hand still. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Open Documents, close it, then open it again. Repeat with staff fading prompts each time. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type the action we use to open an icon. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Staff will name the target only. You attempts the double-click without hand-over-hand support unless needed. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

07. Here is one useful thing to watch for. If it does not open, the mouse probably moved. Keep it still, and try the two clicks again. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

08. Well done. Two quick clicks, mouse held still — you have the double-click, and you know to stay calm and try again. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
