Word: title and alignment
=========================

01. Lesson 12: Word: title and alignment. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we make a page look professional. We will create a clear title, make it bigger and bold, centre it, and then keep the main writing underneath easy to read.

03. Type a short title at the top, then slide the mouse across it so it turns blue. In the toolbar, that little box with a number in it is the text size.

04. Click the size box and choose a bigger number, like twenty-eight. Watch — as the number goes up, your title grows bigger on the page.

05. Now click the bold button, the letter B, so the title is nice and strong.

06. To move it to the middle, look for the align buttons — small rows of lines. Click the centre one, and your title jumps neatly into the middle of the page.

07. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Show a plain sentence and a title. Ask which one is easier to spot and why. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Type a useful title, select it, make it bigger, make it bold and centre it. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type a title for a simple document. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Use alignment buttons to show how text moves. Return the final title to centre. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

08. Here is one useful thing to watch for. Make the title bigger than the writing below, or it will not look like a title. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

09. That looks smart. A big, bold, centred title with your writing underneath — your documents look professional now. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
