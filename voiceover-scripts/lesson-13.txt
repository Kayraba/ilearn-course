Word: lists, spell check and PDF
================================

01. Lesson 13: Word: lists, spell check and PDF. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we make information easier to share. We will use a bulleted list, notice spell-check suggestions, and save as a PDF so the work looks the same on other devices.

03. In the toolbar, look for the bullets button — three little dots stacked with lines beside them. Click it, and a dot appears, ready for your first list item. Type milk.

04. Press Enter after each item and a new dot appears on the next line for you. Milk, bread, eggs — one under the other, tidy and clear.

05. If a word gets a red wavy line under it, that is Word saying: please check the spelling. Right-click the word to see suggested corrections. It is helping you, not telling you off.

06. To share it safely, click File, then Save As. In the box that says "Save as type", click the little arrow to open the list.

07. From that list, choose PDF. A PDF looks exactly the same on every computer, phone or printer. Click Save, and your list is ready to send.

08. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Choose a real list: shopping, medication reminders, jobs, appointments or packing. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Create a bulleted list with at least four items. Practise Enter for a new bullet. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type the file type that keeps a layout the same. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Use Save As and choose PDF. Staff may stop before final saving if using a shared device. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

09. Here is one useful thing to watch for. A red wavy line is a helpful hint, not a mistake to feel bad about. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

10. Great job. Tidy lists, a spell-check to help you, and a PDF that looks the same everywhere — ready to share. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
