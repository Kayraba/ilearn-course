Word: type a sentence
=====================

01. Lesson 9: Word: type a sentence. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we use Word for real writing. A strong sentence starts with a capital letter, has spaces between words, and ends with a full stop. I will show you how to build it slowly.

03. Open Word by double-clicking the blue W, and click onto the blank white page so the cursor is ready.

04. A good sentence starts with a capital letter. We hold the Shift key to make that first capital.

05. We put a space between each word, and we finish with a full stop. There — a clear, complete sentence.

06. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: You open Word, clicks the page, and watches for the flashing cursor. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Build one sentence together: capital letter, words, spaces and full stop. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type a full sentence. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Press Enter and add another sentence. Staff supports punctuation only after the idea is typed. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

07. Here is one useful thing to watch for. A sentence needs a capital at the start and a full stop at the end — it is easy to forget the full stop. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

08. Beautiful writing. A capital to start, spaces between words, a full stop at the end — you wrote a proper sentence. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
