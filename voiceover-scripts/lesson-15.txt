Excel: build a tidy table
=========================

01. Lesson 15: Excel: build a tidy table. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we build a tidy Excel table. We will add headings, type simple data, make the headings stand out, widen a column, and show prices as money.

03. Click cell A1, at the very top-left. Type your first heading, Item, then press Enter to drop down to the cell below.

04. Type your list down the column — bread rolls, orange juice, tea bags. Notice the long words get cut off, because column A is too narrow.

05. To make it wider, move the mouse up to the line between the A and the B, at the top. The pointer changes to a double arrow. Hold the left button down on that line.

06. Now drag to the right, and let go. The column stretches wider, and every word fits. That is how we widen a column whenever text is cut off.

07. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Pick something useful: snacks and prices, bus times, chores, attendance or weekly spending. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Create headings in row 1, then enter at least five rows of simple data. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type a useful table heading. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Enter simple prices and apply currency formatting. Discuss why money formatting helps. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

08. Here is one useful thing to watch for. If a word looks cut off, the column is just too narrow — widen it, do not delete the word. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

09. Excellent. Headings, data underneath, and prices shown as money — a clear table anyone can read. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
