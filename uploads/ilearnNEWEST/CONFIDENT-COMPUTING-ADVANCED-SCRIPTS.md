# Confident Computing - Advanced 60-Minute Tutor Script

This script is designed for one-hour supported lessons. The video should still be
short and clear, but the full lesson is not the video alone. The full lesson is:
watch, demonstrate, practise on a real computer, complete the iLearn activity
cards, record the support level, and reflect.

Use this session rhythm every time:

| Time | Tutor action |
|---:|---|
| 0-5 min | Welcome, check comfort/access needs, remind the learner what they did last time |
| 5-12 min | Watch or present the lesson demonstration |
| 12-25 min | Guided practice with tutor modelling and learner copying |
| 25-45 min | Main learner practice using the iLearn "Guided practice" cards |
| 45-55 min | Independent attempt with fewer prompts |
| 55-60 min | Record prompt level, celebrate progress, say what comes next |

Prompt levels:
- Independent: learner completes the task without help.
- Verbal: learner needs spoken reminders.
- Gesture: learner needs pointing or visual prompts.
- Physical: learner needs hand-over-hand or direct physical support.

## Lesson 1 - Turn on and sign in

Goal: switch the computer on, sign in safely, and recognise the desktop.

Tutor line:
"Today we are learning the full start-up routine. We will turn the computer on,
wait calmly, sign in carefully, and check that the desktop is ready. We are not
rushing. Waiting is part of using a computer well."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Check seating, screen distance, keyboard/mouse position and confidence level.
- 5-12: Demonstrate power button, waiting, sign-in and desktop. Say each step out loud.
- 12-25: Learner repeats the routine with one prompt at a time.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: staff setup, guided routine, waiting decision, sign-in repeat, desktop reflection, shutdown stretch.
- 45-55: Start from signed-out or locked screen and ask learner to complete as much as possible.
- 55-60: Record support level and ask, "What tells us the computer is ready?"

Bottom activities:
1. Prepare the real computer and access setup.
2. Turn on, wait, sign in and identify the desktop.
3. Choose the correct waiting behaviour.
4. Repeat sign-in with fewer prompts.
5. Type one thing seen on the desktop.
6. Stretch: find the safe shut down option.

## Lesson 2 - Use the mouse and click

Goal: move the pointer, click once, and choose an item on screen.

Tutor line:
"The mouse moves the pointer. The pointer is how we reach things on the screen.
Today we will move slowly, stop on the target, and click once to choose."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Warm up hands and check mouse position.
- 5-12: Demonstrate slow pointer movement and one click.
- 12-25: Learner moves to screen corners and clicks named targets.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: corner movement, target clicking, one-click decision, Start menu practice, type "click", independent target round.
- 45-55: Staff names targets only, without pointing unless needed.
- 55-60: Record support level and note mouse control barriers.

Bottom activities:
1. Move pointer to four corners.
2. Click five named targets.
3. Identify one click as "choose".
4. Use Start menu or pinned app practice.
5. Type the word "click".
6. Complete three target clicks with reduced prompting.

## Lesson 3 - Open with double-click

Goal: open a folder or app with two quick clicks.

Tutor line:
"A double-click opens things. The trick is two quick clicks while the mouse stays
still. If it does not open the first time, that is okay. We calmly try again."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Recap one click versus double-click.
- 5-12: Demonstrate click-click rhythm away from icons.
- 12-25: Open and close Documents several times.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: click rhythm, Documents open, double-click decision, Word open/close, type "double-click", independent folder/app round.
- 45-55: Learner opens one folder and one app with fewer prompts.
- 55-60: Record whether difficulty is timing, pointer movement or confidence.

Bottom activities:
1. Practise click-click rhythm.
2. Open Documents repeatedly.
3. Choose what matters during double-click.
4. Open Word and close it.
5. Type the opening action.
6. Open a folder and app independently where possible.

## Lesson 4 - Use the keyboard

Goal: type letters, make spaces, start new lines, and fix mistakes.

Tutor line:
"Today the keyboard becomes a tool for messages. We will type slowly, use Space
between words, Enter for a new line, and Backspace to fix mistakes."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Check keyboard position and whether large text is needed.
- 5-12: Demonstrate cursor, letters, Space, Enter and Backspace.
- 12-25: Learner finds letters and types short words.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: find letters, type three words, Backspace check, two-line practice, type a message, Shift stretch.
- 45-55: Learner types a short message with fewer prompts.
- 55-60: Record which keys are secure and which need more practice.

Bottom activities:
1. Find five letters.
2. Type three short words.
3. Identify Backspace.
4. Create two lines with Enter.
5. Type a short message.
6. Stretch: make one capital with Shift.

## Lesson 5 - Open, minimise and close

Goal: open a program, control the window, and close it safely.

Tutor line:
"A program opens inside a window. The buttons at the top help us manage the
window. X means we are finished with it."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Recap open versus close.
- 5-12: Demonstrate minimise, restore and close.
- 12-25: Learner opens one app and controls the window.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: name buttons, open-close routine, close-button check, tidy screen, type "close", independent open/minimise/restore/close.
- 45-55: Learner follows four window commands with reduced prompts.
- 55-60: Record support level and whether one-window-at-a-time is needed.

Bottom activities:
1. Name the window buttons.
2. Open, minimise, restore and close.
3. Choose X as close.
4. Tidy a screen with multiple windows.
5. Type "close".
6. Complete full window routine independently where possible.

## Lesson 6 - Save a file

Goal: save work with a clear name in Documents.

Tutor line:
"Saving means keeping our work. A good file name helps us find it later. Today we
will make a real note, save it in Documents, close it, and find it again."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Discuss what the learner might want to save.
- 5-12: Demonstrate Save As, file name and Documents.
- 12-25: Learner creates one note and saves with support.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: file name choice, guided save, Documents decision, find again, type a file name, save-again stretch.
- 45-55: Learner saves a second file with fewer prompts.
- 55-60: Record support level and check whether they can explain where the file went.

Bottom activities:
1. Choose a real file name.
2. Save a note in Documents.
3. Choose Documents as save location.
4. Find the file again.
5. Type a useful file name.
6. Stretch: edit and save again.

## Lesson 7 - Find files and folders

Goal: understand files, folders and where saved work is kept.

Tutor line:
"A file is one piece of work. A folder holds files together. Today we will open
Documents, create a practice folder, and place work where it belongs."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Recap saving from Lesson 6.
- 5-12: Demonstrate File Explorer, Documents and folder purpose.
- 12-25: Learner visits Desktop, Documents and Downloads.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: sort examples, folder tour, folder purpose, create Practice Work folder, type folder name, move/save file stretch.
- 45-55: Learner finds the saved file with fewer prompts.
- 55-60: Record support level and note whether folder language is understood.

Bottom activities:
1. Sort file and folder examples.
2. Tour common folders.
3. Choose organised as folder purpose.
4. Create a practice folder.
5. Type a folder name.
6. Stretch: put a file inside the folder.

## Lesson 8 - Practice: save and find

Goal: complete the full create-save-close-find workflow.

Tutor line:
"Today we put the whole routine together. Open Word, type a note, save it, close
it, then find it again. This is where independence starts to show."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Say the full routine before touching the computer.
- 5-12: Tutor demonstrates the complete workflow once.
- 12-25: Learner completes the workflow with support.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: sequence warm-up, guided full routine, save-first check, second attempt, type second note, reflection.
- 45-55: Learner repeats with fewer prompts and a new file name.
- 55-60: Compare prompt level from first attempt to second attempt.

Bottom activities:
1. Say the full routine.
2. Complete create-save-close-find.
3. Choose save before close.
4. Repeat with fewer prompts.
5. Type a second note.
6. Reflect on easiest and hardest step.

## Lesson 9 - Word: type a sentence

Goal: write a complete sentence in Microsoft Word.

Tutor line:
"Now we use Word for real writing. A strong sentence starts with a capital letter,
uses spaces between words, and ends with a full stop."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Recap keyboard and opening Word.
- 5-12: Demonstrate a complete sentence.
- 12-25: Learner opens Word and writes one sentence with support.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: open Word, guided sentence, Shift check, independent sentence, type sentence, second-sentence stretch.
- 45-55: Learner writes a personal sentence with fewer prompts.
- 55-60: Record support needed for cursor, capitals, spaces and punctuation.

Bottom activities:
1. Open Word and place cursor.
2. Build a sentence together.
3. Identify Shift.
4. Write an independent sentence.
5. Type a practice sentence.
6. Stretch: add a second sentence on a new line.

## Lesson 10 - Word: copy, paste and undo

Goal: select text, copy, paste and undo safely.

Tutor line:
"Editing means changing work without starting again. Copy, paste and undo are
three powerful tools. Nothing breaks today; undo can take us back."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Recap selecting text.
- 5-12: Demonstrate Ctrl + C, Ctrl + V and Ctrl + Z.
- 12-25: Learner copies and pastes one word with support.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: select word, guided copy/paste, copy shortcut, undo practice, type paste letter, independent editing challenge.
- 45-55: Learner completes copy-paste-undo sequence with fewer prompts.
- 55-60: Record which shortcut was remembered independently.

Bottom activities:
1. Select a word repeatedly.
2. Copy and paste selected text.
3. Choose Ctrl + C.
4. Practise undo.
5. Type V for paste.
6. Complete independent editing challenge.

## Lesson 11 - Word: format text

Goal: make text clearer using bold, italic, underline and colour.

Tutor line:
"Formatting helps readers notice important words. The rule is choose first, then
change. We select text before pressing B, I, U or colour."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Show plain text versus formatted text.
- 5-12: Demonstrate select first, then format.
- 12-25: Learner applies bold, italic and underline with support.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: selection warm-up, guided formatting set, bold check, colour practice, type rule, independent mini-design.
- 45-55: Learner designs a short note with fewer prompts.
- 55-60: Record whether the learner remembered to select text first.

Bottom activities:
1. Select text before changing it.
2. Apply bold, italic and underline.
3. Choose B for bold.
4. Add readable colour.
5. Type choose first then change.
6. Create a mini-designed note.

## Lesson 14 - Excel: cells, rows and columns

Goal: navigate an Excel spreadsheet using cells, rows and columns.

Tutor line:
"Excel is a grid. Each box is a cell. Columns use letters across the top, rows
use numbers down the side, and the first cell is A1."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Introduce Excel as a grid, not a writing page.
- 5-12: Demonstrate columns, rows and A1.
- 12-25: Learner clicks named cells with support.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: point to rows/columns/cells, guided navigation, cell check, arrow-key practice, type A1, explain cell name stretch.
- 45-55: Learner navigates named cells with fewer prompts.
- 55-60: Record whether they understand letter plus number.

Bottom activities:
1. Point to rows, columns and cells.
2. Click named cells.
3. Choose cell.
4. Move with arrow keys.
5. Type A1.
6. Explain one cell name.

Goal: enter data, use headers, resize columns and format prices.

## Lesson 16 - Excel: formulas and totals

Goal: use formulas and AutoSum to calculate totals.

Tutor line:
"Excel can do maths for us. A formula starts with equals. We can type a simple
sum, use cell names, and use AutoSum to total a whole column."

60-minute lesson plan:
- 0-5: Recap the Excel table from Lesson 15.
- 5-12: Demonstrate =2+3, cell formulas and AutoSum.
- 12-25: Learner types a simple formula with support.
- 25-45: Complete iLearn practice cards: formula confidence, guided formula, equals check, cell formula practice, type =4+6, AutoSum/certificate task.
- 45-55: Learner totals a small price table with fewer prompts.
- 55-60: Complete course, view certificate, and ask what skill they are proud of.

Bottom activities:
1. Understand formulas as instructions.
2. Type a simple formula.
3. Choose equals.
4. Add two cells with a formula.
5. Type =4+6.
6. Use AutoSum and complete certificate.

## Recording Guidance

Do not make each video one hour. Keep the recorded video around 5-8 minutes:
- explain the aim
- show the skill once
- show one common mistake
- tell the learner to pause and complete the practice cards

The one-hour value comes from the supported practice, repetition and evidence
capture. The video is the starter; the iLearn activity block is the lesson.
