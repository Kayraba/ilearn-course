Use the mouse and click
=======================

01. Lesson 2: Use the mouse and click. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we practise using the mouse. Watch the pointer as it moves, stop on the thing you want, then click once. We are aiming for calm, gentle control, not speed.

03. Rest your hand gently on the mouse. As you slide it, watch the little arrow on screen move with you — that arrow is the pointer.

04. The pointer is like our finger, reaching into the screen. We move slowly, and we stop right on the thing we want.

05. To choose something, we rest the pointer on it and press the left button once. Let us choose the blue W for Word.

06. One click chooses it. See how it lights up? We have not opened it yet — one click simply chooses.

07. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Staff will ask you to move slowly to top-left, top-right, bottom-left and bottom-right. Repeat until movement is calmer. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Use real icons or safe blank desktop space. Staff will name a target, You moves to it and single-clicks once. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type the word click. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Staff will say the target name but avoids pointing. Move back to gesture prompts only if you gets stuck. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

08. Here is one useful thing to watch for. Do not press hard or click many times. A light, single click is all it takes. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

09. Lovely work. You can move the pointer and click once to choose — the mouse is your tool now. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
