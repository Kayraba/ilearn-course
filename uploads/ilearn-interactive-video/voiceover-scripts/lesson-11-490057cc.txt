Word: format text
=================

01. Lesson 11: Word: format text. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we make important words stand out. First we choose the text, then we change it. I will show you bold, italic, underline, and colour using that simple rule: choose first, then change.

03. Always choose the word first. Slide the mouse across it so it turns blue. Up in the toolbar at the top is a row of buttons — that is where we change how the word looks.

04. Find the button with a bold letter B. Click it once. Your word becomes bold — darker and stronger, so it stands out.

05. Next to it is the slanted letter I, for italic. Click it and the letters lean over, like this.

06. Now the underlined letter U. Click the U button and a line appears under your word — that is underline, useful for something important.

07. Last is the letter A with a coloured bar under it. Click it to change the colour of your word. Remember the rule: choose the word first, then change it.

08. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: You select a word, then clicks away. Repeat until choose first, change second is clear. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Apply bold, italic and underline to three different words. Undo one and apply it again. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type the rule for formatting. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: You formats a three-line note so the title stands out and key words are readable. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

09. Here is one useful thing to watch for. If nothing changes when you press B, you probably forgot to select the word first. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

10. Lovely. Bold, italic, underline and colour — and you remembered the golden rule: choose first, then change. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
