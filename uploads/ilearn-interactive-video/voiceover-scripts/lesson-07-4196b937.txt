Find files and folders
======================

01. Lesson 7: Find files and folders. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, we organise work. A file is one piece of work, and a folder is a safe place to keep related files together. We will practise putting work where it belongs.

03. Here is one file. Think of a file as a single piece of work — one note, one letter, one list.

04. A folder is like a box that holds many files together, and keeps them tidy. Documents is the folder we use most.

05. So when we look for our work, we open Documents, and there it is, kept safely inside.

06. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Staff will say examples: holiday photo, Documents, CV, shopping folder. You decide file or folder. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Open File Explorer and visit Desktop, Documents and Downloads. Keep returning to Documents as the main place. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type the folder we look in first for saved work. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Use a safe test file. Staff supports you to place it inside the new folder. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

07. Here is one useful thing to watch for. A file is one piece of work; a folder is the box. Do not mix the two up. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

08. Good work. You know a file is one piece of work and a folder holds them together — and you can find your work in Documents. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
