Excel: formulas and totals
==========================

01. Lesson 16: Excel: formulas and totals. I will guide you through it one step at a time.

02. In this lesson, Excel does the maths for us. A formula starts with an equals sign. We will type a simple formula, use cell names, and finish with AutoSum to total a column.

03. We have a short list of prices. Click cell B5, just under the numbers — this is where the total will go.

04. Every formula begins with an equals sign. We type equals, S U M, then the cells to add, in brackets.

05. We press Enter, and Excel adds it all up for us. The total is six. It did the maths, instantly.

06. The AutoSum button, marked with the Greek letter sigma, does this in a single click. A wonderful way to finish the course.

07. When you start the activities, they will follow the same order as this lesson. First, you do a warm-up: Show that formulas are instructions for Excel. Say: equals means Excel, please work this out. Then you answer quick checks, so we know the key words make sense. Next, you practise the real computer skill: Type =2+3, press Enter, then change the numbers and try again. You may also type a short answer, for example: Type a formula to add 2 and 3. At the end, you finish with a stretch or reflection: Return to iLearn, mark the lesson complete, and open the certificate together. Staff will ask you what you are most proud of. Take one activity at a time. If you need help, ask staff, then try the next step yourself.

08. Here is one useful thing to watch for. Forgetting the equals sign is the most common slip. Every formula must start with =. If it happens, pause, reset, and try again calmly.

09. What a finish. Formulas that start with equals, and AutoSum to total a column. You started at the power button and now you write formulas — be proud. Now it is your turn. Scroll down to the activities and do one step at a time. Ask staff for help if you need it — that is part of learning.
