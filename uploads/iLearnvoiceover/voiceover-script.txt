Lesson 01 — Turn on & sign in
Today we are learning the full start-up routine.  (pause)  We will turn the computer on, wait calmly, sign in carefully, and check that the desktop is ready.  (pause)  We are not rushing. Waiting is part of using a computer well.

Lesson 02 — Mouse & click
The mouse moves the pointer. The pointer is how we reach things on the screen.  (pause)  Today we will move slowly, stop on the target, and click once to choose.

Lesson 03 — Double-click
A double-click opens things. The trick is two quick clicks while the mouse stays still.  (pause)  If it does not open the first time, that is okay. We calmly try again.

Lesson 04 — Keyboard
Today the keyboard becomes a tool for messages.  (pause)  We will type slowly, use Space between words, Enter for a new line, and Backspace to fix mistakes.

Lesson 05 — Open & close
A program opens inside a window.  (pause)  The buttons at the top help us manage the window. X means we are finished with it.

Lesson 06 — Save a file
Saving means keeping our work. A good file name helps us find it later.  (pause)  Today we will make a real note, save it in Documents, close it, and find it again.

Lesson 07 — Files & folders
A file is one piece of work. A folder holds files together.  (pause)  Today we will open Documents, create a practice folder, and place work where it belongs.

Lesson 08 — Practice: save & find
Today we put the whole routine together.  (pause)  Open Word, type a note, save it, close it, then find it again. This is where independence starts to show.

Lesson 09 — Word: type a sentence
Now we use Word for real writing.  (pause)  A strong sentence starts with a capital letter, uses spaces between words, and ends with a full stop.

Lesson 10 — Word: copy/paste/undo
Editing means changing work without starting again.  (pause)  Copy, paste and undo are three powerful tools. Nothing breaks today; undo can take us back.

Lesson 11 — Word: bold/italic/colour
Formatting helps readers notice important words.  (pause)  The rule is choose first, then change. We select text before pressing B, I, U or colour.

Lesson 12 — Word: title & alignment
A good title tells the reader what the page is about.  (pause)  We will make the title bigger, bold and centred, then write normal text underneath.

Lesson 13 — Word: lists/spell/PDF
Lists make information easier to read.  (pause)  Spell check helps us improve work, and a PDF is useful because it looks the same when shared or printed.

Lesson 14 — Excel: cells/rows/cols
Excel is a grid. Each box is a cell.  (pause)  Columns use letters across the top, rows use numbers down the side, and the first cell is A1.

Lesson 15 — Excel: tidy table
A table keeps information tidy.  (pause)  Today we will make headings, enter real data, make headers bold, widen columns and format prices as money.

Lesson 16 — Excel: formulas & total
Excel can do maths for us. A formula starts with equals.  (pause)  We can type a simple sum, use cell names, and use AutoSum to total a whole column.
